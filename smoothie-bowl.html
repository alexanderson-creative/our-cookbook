Berry Smoothie Bowl

A recipe by Alex

Prep Time 10 min
Cook Time 0 min
Servings 1

Ingredients

  • For the base:
  • 1 frozen banana
  • 1 cup frozen mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • ¬Ω cup Greek yogurt (or dairy-free alternative)
  • ¬º cup milk of choice
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • For the toppings:
  • Fresh berries
  • Sliced banana
  • 2 tablespoons granola
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds
  • 1 tablespoon almond butter or peanut butter
  • Shredded coconut
  • Drizzle of honey

Instructions

  1. Blend the base: Add frozen banana, frozen berries, Greek yogurt, and milk to a high-powered blender. Blend until thick and creamy, scraping down sides as needed. The texture should be thicker than a regular smoothie‚Äîmore like soft-serve ice cream.
  2. Adjust consistency: If too thick, add a splash more milk. If too thin, add more frozen fruit. You want it thick enough to hold toppings without sinking!
  3. Pour and smooth: Transfer to a bowl and use the back of a spoon to smooth the surface.
  4. Add toppings: Arrange your toppings in rows or sections for that Instagram-worthy look. Start with the granola base, then add fresh fruit, drizzle nut butter, and finish with seeds and coconut.
  5. Serve immediately: Eat right away while it's still thick and cold. This doesn't keep well!

Frozen is key: Use frozen fruit straight from the freezer for the thickest, creamiest texture. Fresh fruit will make it too thin.

Meal prep tip: Freeze banana chunks and pre-portioned berry mixes in bags so you can blend one up in seconds.

Flavor variations: Try mango + pineapple for a tropical bowl, or add a tablespoon of cocoa powder and peanut butter for a chocolate PB version!
