Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

A recipe by Madison

Prep Time 15 min
Cook Time 12 min
Makes 24 cookies

  • 2 large eggs, room temperature

  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • Flaky sea salt for topping

Instructions

  1. Prep the dry ingredients: Whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl. Set aside.
  2. Cream the butter and sugars: In a large bowl or stand mixer, beat softened butter with both sugars until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. Scrape down the sides halfway through.
  3. Add the eggs: Beat in eggs one at a time, then add vanilla. Mix until well combined.
  4. Combine wet and dry: Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients, mixing on low speed until just combined. Don't overmix!
  5. Fold in the good stuff: Stir in chocolate chips (and walnuts if using) with a wooden spoon or spatula.
  6. Chill the dough: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 72 hours‚Äîthe flavor actually improves!)
  7. Bake: Preheat oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C). Scoop rounded tablespoons of dough onto parchment-lined baking sheets, spacing 2 inches apart. Sprinkle with flaky salt.
  8. The perfect bake: Bake for 10-12 minutes until edges are golden but centers look slightly underdone. They'll firm up as they cool. Let rest on pan for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

The secret is chilling: Cold dough = thicker cookies that don't spread too much. For extra thick cookies, chill overnight!

Brown butter variation: For an extra nutty, caramel flavor, brown the butter first and chill it until solid before creaming.

Storage: Keep in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. Freeze baked cookies for up to 3 months, or freeze dough balls and bake straight from frozen (add 2 minutes to bake time).
