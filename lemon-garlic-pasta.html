Lemon Garlic Pasta

A recipe by Brian

Prep Time 5 min
Cook Time 15 min
Servings 4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti or linguine
  • 6 tablespoons butter, divided
  • 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • Juice of 1Â½ lemons (about Â¼ cup)
  • Â½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
  • Â¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Â½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup reserved pasta water

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Before draining, reserve 1 cup of pasta water.
  2. Toast the garlic: While pasta cooks, melt 4 tablespoons of butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add sliced garlic and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently, until golden and fragrant. Don't let it burn!
  3. Build the sauce: Add lemon zest and red pepper flakes (if using) to the garlic butter. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Combine everything: Add drained pasta to the skillet along with lemon juice and remaining 2 tablespoons butter. Toss well, adding pasta water a splash at a time until you get a silky sauce that coats the noodles.
  5. Finish and serve: Remove from heat, add Parmesan and parsley, and toss to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan on top.
